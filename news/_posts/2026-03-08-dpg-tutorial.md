In the tutorial titled [Linking large language models with digital workflows for materials science simulations](https://www.dpg-verhandlungen.de/year/2026/conference/dresden/part/tut/session/6/contribution/1)
the pyiron developers presented the recent advances of the pyiron workflow framework, with a specific focous on the 
integration of large language models (LLM) to accelerate the workflow development. 

The tutorial [website](http://workshop.pyiron.org/DPG-tutorial-2026/) summarizes the topics covered in the tutorial and
the corresponding content is available in the [Github repository](https://github.com/pyiron-workshop/DPG-tutorial-2026).

In comparison to generating Python code directly with an LLM the advantage of using a workflow framework is the 
restriction of the flexibility of to focus on the generation of nodes and edges which reduces the risk of hallucination.
